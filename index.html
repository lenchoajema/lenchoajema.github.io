Profile

Reading the physical world through sensors

I build systems that turn raw sensor streams into measurements of the world — where something moved, how fast, and what that means. My MSc thesis used deep learning to detect and classify events in UAV aerial footage; my current work extends that toward debris-flow monitoring, road-safety sensing, and network security research at the doctoral level. Before all of this, I spent seven years as a computer engineer in a defence environment and three years teaching AI and computer vision at university — both of which shape how I think about building systems that have to work outside the lab.

Research focus

Six problems I keep returning to

01

Optical flow & motion estimation

Recovering dense motion fields between frames — the same underlying math whether the frames come from a phone camera or a mountainside LiDAR scanner.

02

Object detection & tracking

Finding and following the things that matter in a scene, under real-world conditions: occlusion, poor light, and motion blur.

03

Remote sensing

Reading terrain and change from satellite and aerial sensors — multispectral imagery, SAR, and point cloud data.

04

Sensor fusion

Combining camera, LiDAR, and radar data so that each sensor's strengths cover for the others' blind spots.

05

Explainable AI

Model decisions that a geologist, safety engineer, or auditor can actually inspect, trust, and question.

06

ML for network security

Applying sequence models and anomaly detection to traffic and log data — deep learning meeting years of systems engineering.
